## More Examples of Good Content

```
BBNN Investigation reveals that presidential candidate Mr. Krabs has a troubling
history of workplace violations. Documents obtained by our investigative team show
that the Krusty Krab has been cited 47 times by the health department over the past
decade for various code violations. While the candidate touts his business acumen,
our investigation raises questions about whether his management style prioritizes
profit over people's wellbeing.

```

### Good Human Interest Story
```
The crowd fell silent as SpongeBob SquarePants knelt down to talk to 8-year-old
Tommy, a young starfish who had been waiting for hours to meet the presidential
candidate. "My dad lost his job," Tommy said quietly, "and now we might lose our

promised to help however he could. It's a moment that reveals something the polls
and policy papers can't: who SpongeBob SquarePants really is when the cameras
aren't rolling.
```

## Tips for Writing Great Content

1. **Be Specific**
   - Bad: "The candidate talked about healthcare"
   - Good: "The candidate proposed expanding Medicare to cover all residents"

2. **Use Real Quotes**
   - Bad: "He said he would help people"
   - Good: "As he told the crowd, 'Every citizen deserves access to healthcare'"

3. **Add Details**
   - Bad: "Many people attended"
   - Good: "Over 500 supporters packed the Goo Lagoon amphitheater"

4. **Show, Don't Just Tell**
   - Bad: "She was nervous"
   - Good: "Sandy's hands trembled slightly as she adjusted her helmet"

5. **Balance is Key**
   - Include both positive and critical perspectives
   - Give both campaigns fair coverage
   - Present facts, then analyze
