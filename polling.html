Election Polling Data

Tracking public opinion and candidate standings

Current Poll Results

Head-to-Head Matchup

  • SpongeBob & Patrick: 83.9%
  • Mr. Krabs & Sandy: 16.1%

Leadership Qualities

  • Childish but kind (SpongeBob): 74.2%
  • Smart but money-focused (Mr. Krabs): 25.8%
  • Slow but loyal (Patrick): 80.6%
  • Inventive but short-tempered (Sandy): 19.4%

Key Issue Snapshot

  • Economy: Social welfare & lower tariffs (Patty Party) lead over big-business focus (Krabby Party).
  • Second Amendment: 89.7% favor background checks, a do-not-sell list, and a special license; minimal support for eliminating gun controls.
  • Healthcare: 83.3% support government-funded care for all versus 11.1% for individual-only coverage.
  • Education: Future-focused K–12 and career skills (Patty) paired with creativity/competition (Krabby) drew most support.
  • Environment: Rejoining the Paris Agreement topped environmental choices.
  • Criminal Justice: Mental health resources were the most popular policy at 84.6%; decriminalizing minor offenses received 15.4% support.
  • Immigration: Supporting ICE victims and easing citizenship (Krabby) earned 71.4% support.
  • Nuclear Testing: Voters favored active aid for victims and limiting future testing over passive recovery approaches.

Poll Methodology

Number of Respondents: 31

Polling Period: November 27 – December 4, 2025 (reported at 2:05 p.m. on Dec. 4)

Method: Online survey distributed to high school voters via Google Forms; responses collected anonymously.

Margin of Error: ±17.6% at 95% confidence, given sample size.

Polling Questions

Core prompts asked in the survey:

  • Which ticket would you choose for president and vice president if the election were today?
  • Which leadership qualities resonate more with you for president and for vice president?
  • Which economic approach do you favor: big-business incentives or expanded social welfare with lower tariffs?
  • Which gun policy do you support: eliminating controls or adding background checks, a do-not-sell list, and special licensing?
  • Should healthcare be government-funded for all or purchased individually without employer requirements?
  • Where do you stand on abortion access: removing barriers entirely or protecting access up to fetal viability?
  • What education priorities matter most: career-focused K–12 and subsidized higher ed or family-directed, competitive schooling?
  • Which environmental action should come first: rejoining the Paris Agreement, emissions limits, or tax incentives for sustainable business?
  • How should we reform criminal justice: mental health investment and alternative sentencing or traditional incarceration approaches?
  • Which immigration policy do you favor: easing citizenship and supporting ICE victims or stricter removals for undocumented criminals?
  • What response to nuclear testing do you support: medical aid and environmental restoration or allowing natural recovery without new bans?

Candidate Responses to Poll Results

Patty Party Response

A Patty Party spokesperson celebrated SpongeBob and Patrick capturing 85.2% of the vote, noting the ticket’s kindness-first platform. When asked about the weak showing for decriminalizing minor offenses (15.4%), the campaign said they will emphasize safeguards and pair diversion with the widely supported expansion of mental health resources (84.6%) to reassure safety-minded voters.

Krabby Party Response

The Krabby Party acknowledged trailing with 14.8% support and recognized that their individual-purchase healthcare plan drew only 11.1% approval. They signaled openness to blending employer incentives and cost caps while keeping market choice, aiming to regain voters who favored the Patty Party’s government-funded approach (81.5%).

Analysis

SpongeBob and Patrick hold a commanding lead fueled by personal likability and policies tied to government-backed support (healthcare, social welfare, and environmental commitments). Voters favor regulation and safety over laissez-faire approaches on guns, healthcare, and nuclear issues. Economic and immigration preferences show some appetite for the Krabby ticket’s pro-business and streamlined citizenship ideas, suggesting room for movement if the campaign pivots toward affordability and collaboration. The biggest red flags for each side are the Patty Party’s decriminalization plank and the Krabby Party’s healthcare stance—areas where nuance and compromise could meaningfully shift future tracking polls.
